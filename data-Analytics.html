Data & Analytics Learning Roadmap

Step-by-Step Learning Path

  1. Month 1-2: SQL fundamentals - database queries, joins, aggregations
  2. Month 2-3: Excel mastery - pivot tables, formulas, advanced functions
  3. Month 3-4: Data visualization - Tableau/Power BI basics
  4. Month 4-5: Python basics - data manipulation with pandas, NumPy
  5. Month 5-6: Statistics - probability, hypothesis testing, distributions
  6. Month 6-7: Choose specialization - Analytics, Science, or Business
  7. Month 7-9: Advanced topics - Machine learning or deep domain knowledge
  8. Month 9-10: Build projects - portfolio building
  9. Month 10+: Deploy & get job

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between Data Analyst and Data Scientist?

Data Analysts focus on analyzing existing data and creating reports. Data Scientists build predictive models using machine learning. Analysts use SQL and visualization, while Scientists use programming and statistics heavily.

What does a Business Analyst do?

Business Analysts work with stakeholders to understand business needs and requirements. They bridge the gap between business teams and technical teams. They don't necessarily build models but help define what solutions are needed.

Which is easier to start - Data Analyst or Data Scientist?

Data Analyst is easier - you need SQL, Excel, and visualization skills. Data Scientist requires Python, Machine Learning, and advanced statistics. Most people start as Data Analyst and move to Data Scientist.

How long does it take to learn Data Analytics?

Basics: 3-4 months. Intermediate: 6-8 months. Professional level: 1-2 years. It depends on your background and daily practice time.

Do I need a statistics degree for Data Analytics?

No. Many successful data professionals are self-taught. What matters is your practical skills, portfolio, and ability to solve business problems. Online courses are enough.

What tools should I learn first?

Start with SQL and Excel - they are most essential. Then learn a visualization tool (Tableau or Power BI). Then Python for more advanced analysis. This progression will help you build stronger foundations.

What companies hire Data professionals in India?

Amazon, Flipkart, Google, Microsoft, Facebook, LinkedIn, Airbnb, Netflix, and many startups. All companies need data professionals. Remote jobs are abundant.

What's the salary growth in Data Analytics?

Fresher: ₹3-5 LPA. 2-3 years: ₹6-10 LPA. 5+ years: ₹15-25 LPA. With specialization (Data Science), you can earn more.
